# My Portfolio Reflection

Creating this artifact helped deepen my understanding of how machine learning algorithms relate to specific application domains.

While studying artificial intelligence, it is easy to focus only on the algorithms themselves. However, in real-world AI systems, selecting the appropriate algorithm depends largely on the nature of the data and the problem being solved.

For example, tree-based models such as Random Forests and Gradient Boosting often perform extremely well on structured tabular datasets. In contrast, convolutional neural networks are specifically designed to process visual data, making them highly effective for computer vision tasks such as object detection and medical imaging.

Another important development in recent years is the rise of transformer-based architectures, which have dramatically improved natural language processing tasks and enabled modern generative AI systems such as large language models.

Developing this visual framework reinforced my understanding of how algorithm selection connects to practical AI applications. As I continue my studies in artificial intelligence, this perspective will help guide my approach to designing AI solutions for real-world problems.
